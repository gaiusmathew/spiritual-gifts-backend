Spiritual Gifts Discovery Tool (Serving/ Doing Gifts)
Gift of Service:
1.	I gladly step in to meet the physical needs of the church by working behind the scenes. 
2.	I joyfully volunteer for tasks such as cleaning, arranging, serving food, organizing materials, managing logistics etc.
3.	I am comfortable working quietly and supportively in the background without being in the spotlight and without getting noticed.
4.	I am a program-oriented person and stay committed to the task even when it is tedious and routine.  
5.	I feel satisfaction when a ministry event or church activity runs well, because of how I helped behind-the-scenes.
Gifts of Help:
1.	I am a people person and I’m sensitive to the suffering and needy individuals in the church.
2.	I love to be with people and help people than spending my time in arranging a place for church gathering. 
3.	I willingly sacrifice my time, comfort, and resources to personally help others in the church. 
4.	I can easily sense when someone is overwhelmed or stressed and I help them without being asked for. 
5.	When someone is sick or hurting, I naturally feel led to visit them and support them. 
Gift of Leadership:
1.	I can see the bigger picture for the church and sense the direction God wants us to move. 
2.	As a spiritual visionary, I enjoy inspiring others with a clear vision of what we can achieve together as a church.
3.	When I talk to people, I try to provide vision and spiritual direction to them. 
4.	When a project or ministry is stagnant, I’m motivated to bring fresh energy and direction.
5.	I focus more on people’s growth, unity, and direction than on managing administrative work. 
Gift of Administration
1.	I enjoy planning and organizing people and resources to help the church achieve its ministry goals. 
2.	I can take a complex church and ministry projects and break it into clear, manageable steps.
3.	I find satisfaction in planning programs, coordinating events, and keeping things running smoothly.
4.	I handle administrative tasks patiently because I see the importance of good systems and processes. 
5.	Others often rely on me to plan and manage special church events like outings or Christmas programs. 
Gift of Giving:
1.	I feel a deep joy and satisfaction when I offer financial help to someone in need, often anonymously.
2.	I am quick to respond when I hear about a genuine financial need, even if it requires personal sacrifice.
3.	I am willing to lower my own standard of living so that I can give more to God’s work.
4.	I often look for ways to financially support ministries, missionaries, or people serving God. 
5.	I am able to budget and manage my finances carefully so that I can give more generously.
Gift of Mercy:
1.	I am patient and gentle when caring for people who are weak, sick, old or discouraged.
2.	I feel drawn to help those who are poor or overlooked and rejected by others.
3.	I feel a deep spiritual satisfaction when I show mercy and kindness to others.
4.	I like to visit places like orphanages and old age home and spent time with them. 
5.	When I hear about people in pain, hunger and suffering, I am deeply moved with compassion. 
Gift of faith:
1.	I have strong confidence in God and His promises, even when others around me doubt.
2.	Even when I do not know all the details, I find it easy to move forward when I sense God’s leading.  
3.	Lack of funds or resources does not stop me in pursuing what God has called me to do. 
4.	I believe God is faithful to provide, and take courageous steps in life and ministry, not looking at my bank balance. 
5.	I pray bold prayers, often for impossible situations, believing that nothing is too hard for God.
Gift of Hospitality:
1.	I find joy in welcoming people into my home or personal space.
2.	I naturally make others feel comfortable, accepted, and cared for when they visit me.
3.	I enjoy preparing food, creating a warm atmosphere, and planning gatherings at my home.
4.	I feel energized, not burdened, when I have the opportunity to host others at my home.
5.	I take the initiative to invite people to my home who are new to the church.
